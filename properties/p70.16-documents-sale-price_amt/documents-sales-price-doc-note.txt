### P70.16 documents sale price amount

**Property**: gmn:P70_16_documents_sale_price_amount
**Type**: Datatype Property
**Domain**: gmn:E31_2_Sales_Contract
**Range**: xsd:decimal
**Label**: "P70.16 documents sale price amount"

This property allows you to record the numeric monetary amount of a sale price documented in a sales contract. Use this property together with P70.17 (documents sale price currency) to specify complete monetary values.

**Important**: Always record amounts as decimal numbers without currency symbols or thousands separators. For example: 1500.50 (not €1,500.50 or 1.500,50).

The property represents the full CIDOC-CRM path:
E31_Document → P70_documents → E8_Acquisition → P177_assigned_property_of_type → E97_Monetary_Amount → P181_has_amount → xsd:decimal

**When to use**: When a sales contract explicitly states a monetary amount for the sale price.

**Required companion**: P70.17 documents sale price currency (to specify the currency type)

**Examples**:
- 1500.50 (one thousand five hundred lira and 10 soldi)
- 25000.00 (twenty-five thousand lira exactly)
- 345.75 (three hundred forty-five lira and 15 soldi)

## Recording Sale Prices

### Overview
Sale prices consist of two components that must be recorded together:
1. **Amount** (P70_16): The numeric value (e.g., 1500.50)
2. **Currency** (P70_17): The currency type (e.g., Lira Genovese)

### Step-by-Step Instructions

1. **Locate the price in the source document**
   - Look for phrases like "pro pretio librarum" (for the price of lira)
   - Check for amounts in numbers or written out
   - Note both the quantity and the currency mentioned

2. **Record the numeric amount**
   - Use field: P70_16 documents sale price amount
   - Enter as a decimal number: 1500.50
   - Do NOT include:
     * Currency symbols (€, £, L)
     * Thousands separators (1,500 or 1.500)
     * Words (lire, soldi)
   - DO include:
     * Decimal point for fractional amounts (1500.50)
     * All decimal places shown in source (preserve precision)

3. **Record the currency**
   - Use field: P70_17 documents sale price currency
   - Select from controlled vocabulary or enter URI
   - Common currencies: Lira Genovese, Florin, Ducat

### Examples from Historical Documents

**Example 1**: Simple sale price
```
Source text: "pro pretio librarum mille quingentarum"
             (for the price of one thousand five hundred lira)

Field P70_16: 1500.00
Field P70_17: Lira Genovese
```

**Example 2**: Price with fractional currency
```
Source text: "pro pretio librarum trecentarum quadraginta quinque et solidorum quindecim"
             (for the price of three hundred forty-five lira and fifteen soldi)

Field P70_16: 345.75
Field P70_17: Lira Genovese

Note: 15 soldi = 0.75 lira (since 1 lira = 20 soldi)
```

**Example 3**: Large transaction
```
Source text: "pro pretio librarum viginti quinque milium"
             (for the price of twenty-five thousand lira)

Field P70_16: 25000.00
Field P70_17: Lira Genovese
```

### Converting Historical Currency Units

Genoese monetary system:
- 1 Lira = 20 Soldi = 240 Denari
- 1 Soldo = 12 Denari

Conversion to decimal:
- 10 soldi = 0.50 lira
- 15 soldi = 0.75 lira
- 5 soldi = 0.25 lira
- 6 denari = 0.025 lira (6/240)

**Example conversions**:
| Source Format | Decimal Entry | Explanation |
|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| 100 lire | 100.00 | Exact lire |
| 100 lire 10 soldi | 100.50 | 10÷20 = 0.50 |
| 100 lire 15 soldi | 100.75 | 15÷20 = 0.75 |
| 100 lire 5 soldi | 100.25 | 5÷20 = 0.25 |
| 100 lire 6 denari | 100.025 | 6÷240 = 0.025 |

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Including currency symbol: €1500.50
✅ Correct format: 1500.50

❌ Using thousands separator: 1,500.50
✅ Correct format: 1500.50

❌ Rounding unnecessarily: 1500
✅ Preserve decimals: 1500.00

❌ Entering only amount (no currency)
✅ Enter both amount AND currency

❌ Converting to modern currency
✅ Record original historical currency

### Quality Check

Before submitting, verify:
- [ ] Amount is a decimal number
- [ ] No currency symbols or separators
- [ ] Decimal precision matches source
- [ ] Currency is specified
- [ ] Both amount and currency fields are completed

## Monetary Values in Genoese Contracts

### Price Ranges (15th Century)

Understanding typical price ranges helps validate data entry:

**Real Estate**:
- Small urban house: 500-2,000 lire
- Large merchant house: 3,000-10,000 lire
- Palazzo (noble residence): 10,000-100,000 lire
- Shop/bottega: 2,000-10,000 lire
- Vineyard: 1,000-5,000 lire
- Agricultural land: 500-3,000 lire

**Moveable Property**:
- Silk (per bale): 20-50 lire
- Wool cloth (per piece): 10-30 lire
- Books (manuscript): 5-50 lire
- Ships (merchant vessel): 5,000-50,000 lire
- Furniture (complete household): 50-500 lire

**Other**:
- Enslaved person: 50-200 lire (historical documentation)
- Dowry (modest): 500-2,000 lire
- Dowry (wealthy family): 5,000-30,000 lire

### Currency Context

**Lira Genovese**: 
- Primary accounting unit in Genoa
- Subdivided into 20 soldi or 240 denari
- Used for most domestic transactions

**Florin**:
- Gold coin used in international trade
- More stable value than lira
- Preferred for large transactions

**Ducat**:
- Venetian gold coin
- Widely accepted in Mediterranean trade
- Sometimes specified in Genoese contracts

### Research Applications

Precise monetary data enables:

1. **Price Analysis**: Track inflation and deflation over time
2. **Economic Networks**: Map wealth flows between families
3. **Property Valuation**: Compare relative values of different properties
4. **Social Stratification**: Analyze wealth distribution
5. **Market Studies**: Understand supply and demand dynamics
6. **Family Wealth**: Track economic status across generations

## Common Issues: Sale Price Amount

### Issue: "Amount not saving"

**Possible causes**:
1. Invalid decimal format (using comma instead of period)
2. Including currency symbols
3. Non-numeric characters in amount field

**Solution**: Enter only numeric values with decimal point, e.g., 1500.50

---

### Issue: "Currency required error"

**Cause**: Amount entered without currency

**Solution**: Always specify both P70_16 (amount) and P70_17 (currency)

---

### Issue: "Precision lost"

**Cause**: Using wrong number format or rounding

**Solution**: Use decimal format and preserve all decimal places from source:
- Wrong: 1500 (loses .00)
- Right: 1500.00

---

### Issue: "Multiple amounts for one contract"

**Situation**: Contract mentions deposit, final payment, and total

**Options**:
1. Enter only the total amount in P70_16
2. Add note explaining payment structure in comments
3. Consider modeling as separate transactions if appropriate

**Example**:
```
Amount (P70_16): 2000.00  (total)
Notes: "Contract specifies 500 lire deposit paid immediately, 
        balance of 1500 lire due at delivery"
```

---

### Issue: "Currency conversion in source"

**Situation**: Source states "1000 lire or 200 florins"

**Solution**: 
- Record the primary currency mentioned first
- Add note about alternative currency
- Don't convert; record as stated

**Example**:
```
Amount (P70_16): 1000.00
Currency (P70_17): Lira Genovese
Notes: "Contract also states equivalent value of 200 florins"
```
