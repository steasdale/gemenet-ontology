### P70.22 indicates receiving party (updated)

**Property URI**: `gmn:P70_22_indicates_receiving_party`

**Label**: "P70.22 indicates receiving party" (English)

**Domain**: Union of `gmn:E31_4_Cession_of_Rights_Contract`, `gmn:E31_5_Declaration`, `gmn:E31_7_Donation_Contract`, `gmn:E31_8_Dowry_Contract`

**Range**: `cidoc:E39_Actor`

**Superproperty**: `cidoc:P70_documents`

**Definition**: Simplified property for associating a document with the person or entity receiving something in the documented activity. In donation contracts, this is the donee receiving the donated property.

**CIDOC-CRM Path for Donations**:
```
E31_Document > P70_documents > E8_Acquisition > P22_transferred_title_to > E39_Actor
```

**Inverse**: `P22i_acquired_title_through`

**Quantification**: Many to many (0,n:0,n)

**Scope Note**: The receiving party in a donation is the donee or beneficiary who acquires ownership of the donated property without providing payment. This party may be an individual, a religious institution, a charitable organization, or any legal entity capable of holding property. The property is used for acquisitions where actual ownership is transferred.

**Examples**:
- Hospital Pammatone receiving a warehouse donation
- Monastery of St. Francis receiving vineyard property
- Family member receiving gifted real estate
- Charitable institution receiving endowment property

---

### Example 1: Basic Donation with P70.22

**Input (shortcut):**
```turtle
<donation001> a gmn:E31_7_Donation_Contract ;
    gmn:P70_32_indicates_donor <widow_maria> ;
    gmn:P70_22_indicates_receiving_party <hospital_pammatone> ;
    gmn:P70_33_indicates_object_of_donation <warehouse_porto> .
```

**Output (CIDOC-CRM compliant):**
```turtle
<donation001> a gmn:E31_7_Donation_Contract ;
    cidoc:P70_documents <donation001/acquisition> .

<donation001/acquisition> a cidoc:E8_Acquisition ;
    cidoc:P23_transferred_title_from <widow_maria> ;
    cidoc:P22_transferred_title_to <hospital_pammatone> ;
    cidoc:P24_transferred_title_of <warehouse_porto> .
```

**Explanation**: The E8_Acquisition node connects the donor (P23), receiving party/donee (P22), and the donated property (P24), forming a complete ownership transfer structure.

### P70.22 indicates receiving party (updated for dowries)

**Property URI**: `gmn:P70_22_indicates_receiving_party`

**Label**: "P70.22 indicates receiving party" (English)

**Domain**: Union of `gmn:E31_4_Cession_of_Rights_Contract`, `gmn:E31_5_Declaration`, `gmn:E31_7_Donation_Contract`, `gmn:E31_8_Dowry_Contract`

**Range**: `cidoc:E39_Actor`

**Superproperty**: `cidoc:P70_documents`

**Definition**: Simplified property for associating a document with the person or entity receiving something in the documented activity. In dowry contracts, this is the party (often the spouse or the couple) receiving the dowry property.

**CIDOC-CRM Path for Dowries**:
```
E31_Document > P70_documents > E8_Acquisition > P22_transferred_title_to > E39_Actor
```

**Inverse**: `P22i_acquired_title_through`

**Quantification**: Many to many (0,n:0,n)

**Scope Note**: The receiving party in a dowry contract is typically the husband or the married couple who acquire ownership of the dowry property. This is distinguished from the donor (often a parent or family member). The dowry receiving party becomes the legal owner of the transferred property as part of the marriage arrangement.

**Examples**:
- Husband receiving house as dowry from wife's father
- Married couple jointly receiving dowry property
- Spouse receiving moveable property as part of marriage settlement
- Son-in-law receiving land parcel from bride's family

### P70.22 indicates receiving party (for cessions)

**Property URI**: `gmn:P70_22_indicates_receiving_party`

**Label**: "P70.22 indicates receiving party" (English)

**Domain**: Union of `gmn:E31_4_Cession_of_Rights_Contract`, `gmn:E31_5_Declaration`, `gmn:E31_7_Donation_Contract`, `gmn:E31_8_Dowry_Contract`

**Range**: `cidoc:E39_Actor`

**Superproperty**: `cidoc:P70_documents`

**Definition**: Simplified property for associating a document with the person or entity receiving something in the documented activity. In cession of rights contracts, this is the party receiving the ceded rights.

**CIDOC-CRM Path for Cessions**:
```
E31_Document > P70_documents > E7_Activity > P14_carried_out_by > E39_Actor
```

**Inverse**: `P14i_performed`

**Quantification**: Many to many (0,n:0,n)

**Scope Note**: In cession contracts, both the conceding party and receiving party are linked via P14_carried_out_by, modeling the cession as a bilateral activity where both parties actively participate in the rights transfer. The receiving party is the actor who acquires the legal rights being transferred.

**Examples**:
- Merchant acquiring debt collection rights
- Business partner receiving usufruct rights
- Creditor receiving assigned contract claims
- Heir receiving transferred inheritance rights

---

### Example: Cession of Debt Collection Rights

**Input (shortcut):**
```turtle
<cession001> a gmn:E31_4_Cession_of_Rights_Contract ;
    gmn:P70_21_indicates_conceding_party <merchant_marco> ;
    gmn:P70_22_indicates_receiving_party <merchant_pietro> ;
    gmn:P70_23_indicates_object_of_cession <debt_claim_001> .
```

**Output (CIDOC-CRM compliant):**
```turtle
<cession001> a gmn:E31_4_Cession_of_Rights_Contract ;
    cidoc:P70_documents <cession001/cession> .

<cession001/cession> a cidoc:E7_Activity ;
    cidoc:P2_has_type <http://vocab.getty.edu/aat/300417639> ;  # transfers of rights
    cidoc:P14_carried_out_by <merchant_marco> ;
    cidoc:P14_carried_out_by <merchant_pietro> ;
    cidoc:P16_used_specific_object <debt_claim_001> .
```

**Explanation**: Both parties appear as P14_carried_out_by participants in the cession activity, reflecting the bilateral nature of rights transfers. The legal object (rights) is connected via P16_used_specific_object.

---

### Cession Property Relationships

| Property | Label | CIDOC Path | Semantic Role |
|----------|-------|------------|---------------|
| P70.21 | indicates conceding party | P14_carried_out_by | Rights transferor |
| **P70.22** | **indicates receiving party** | **P14_carried_out_by** | **Rights acquirer** |
| P70.23 | indicates object of cession | P16_used_specific_object | Rights being transferred |

**Note**: Unlike donations (which use P23/P22 for asymmetric transfer), cessions use P14 for both parties to represent mutual participation.

### P70.22 indicates receiving party (for declarations)

**Property URI**: `gmn:P70_22_indicates_receiving_party`

**Label**: "P70.22 indicates receiving party" (English)

**Domain**: Union of `gmn:E31_4_Cession_of_Rights_Contract`, `gmn:E31_5_Declaration`, `gmn:E31_7_Donation_Contract`, `gmn:E31_8_Dowry_Contract`

**Range**: `cidoc:E39_Actor`

**Superproperty**: `cidoc:P70_documents`

**Definition**: Simplified property for associating a document with the person or entity receiving something in the documented activity. In declarations, this is the party to whom the declaration is addressed or directed.

**CIDOC-CRM Path for Declarations**:
```
E31_Document > P70_documents > E7_Activity > P01_has_domain > E39_Actor
```

**Inverse**: `P01i_is_domain_of`

**Quantification**: Many to many (0,n:0,n)

**Scope Note**: The receiving party in a declaration is the addressee or target of the declaration - the party to whom the statement is directed. This is semantically distinct from the declarant (who carries out the declaration via P14_carried_out_by). The use of P01_has_domain establishes the receiving party as the "subject" or target of the declaration activity.

**Examples**:
- Magistrate receiving debt acknowledgment
- Official receiving formal statement
- Creditor addressed in debt declaration
- Authority figure receiving sworn testimony

---

### Example: Declaration to Magistrate

**Input (shortcut):**
```turtle
<declaration001> a gmn:E31_5_Declaration ;
    gmn:P70_24_indicates_declarant <merchant_pietro> ;
    gmn:P70_22_indicates_receiving_party <magistrate_antonio> ;
    gmn:P70_25_indicates_declaration_subject <debt_acknowledgment> .
```

**Output (CIDOC-CRM compliant):**
```turtle
<declaration001> a gmn:E31_5_Declaration ;
    cidoc:P70_documents <declaration001/declaration> .

<declaration001/declaration> a cidoc:E7_Activity ;
    cidoc:P2_has_type <http://vocab.getty.edu/aat/300027623> ;  # declarations
    cidoc:P14_carried_out_by <merchant_pietro> ;
    cidoc:P01_has_domain <magistrate_antonio> ;
    cidoc:P16_used_specific_object <debt_acknowledgment> .
```

**Explanation**: The declarant (Pietro) carries out the act (P14), while the receiving party (Antonio) is the target (P01). This asymmetric structure captures the directed nature of declarations.

---

### Declaration Property Relationships

| Property | Label | CIDOC Path | Semantic Role | Property Type |
|----------|-------|------------|---------------|---------------|
| P70.24 | indicates declarant | P14_carried_out_by | Speaker/maker | Active participant |
| **P70.22** | **indicates receiving party** | **P01_has_domain** | **Addressee/target** | **Passive recipient** |
| P70.25 | indicates declaration subject | P16_used_specific_object | Subject matter | Object of statement |

### P70.22 Across All Document Types

This table shows how P70.22 adapts to different document contexts:

| Document Type | Receiving Party Role | CIDOC Event Class | CIDOC Property | Complementary Party Property | Object Property |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------------|----------------|------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Cession** | Rights acquirer | E7_Activity | P14_carried_out_by | P70.21 (conceding party) | P70.23 (rights object) |
| **Declaration** | Addressee/target | E7_Activity | P01_has_domain | P70.24 (declarant) | P70.25 (subject) |
| **Donation** | Donee/beneficiary | E8_Acquisition | P22_transferred_title_to | P70.32 (donor) | P70.33 (donated object) |
| **Dowry** | Receiving spouse | E8_Acquisition | P22_transferred_title_to | P70.32 (donor) | P70.34 (dowry object) |

---

### Transformation Logic Summary

```
IF document type = Donation OR Dowry:
    USE: E8_Acquisition with P22_transferred_title_to
    REASON: Actual ownership transfer

ELSE IF document type = Declaration:
    USE: E7_Activity with P01_has_domain
    REASON: Communication/directed statement

ELSE IF document type = Cession:
    USE: E7_Activity with P14_carried_out_by
    REASON: Bilateral rights transfer

```

SECTION 6: USAGE GUIDELINES FOR DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

### When to Use P70.22

**Use P70.22** when:
- Documenting the recipient of donated property
- Recording the spouse receiving dowry property
- Identifying the party acquiring ceded rights
- Specifying the addressee of a declaration

**Do NOT use P70.22** for:
- The party transferring property (use P70.32 for donations/dowries, P70.21 for cessions)
- The party making a declaration (use P70.24)
- Witnesses or notaries (use appropriate witness properties)
- Property being transferred (use P70.33, P70.34, or P70.23)

---

### Data Entry Best Practices

1. **Always specify document type first**: The @type determines which transformation path is used
2. **Use consistent URIs**: Ensure actor URIs are consistent across properties
3. **Provide complete data**: Include complementary properties (donor, object, etc.)
4. **Multiple recipients**: Use array format for joint recipients
5. **Actor typing**: Actors will be automatically typed as E39_Actor during transformation

---

SECTION 7: IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR DOCUMENTATION

### Technical Considerations

1. **Shared Event Nodes**: All related properties for a document reference the same E7_Activity or E8_Acquisition node
2. **URI Generation**: Event URIs follow pattern: `{document_uri}/acquisition` or `{document_uri}/declaration` or `{document_uri}/cession`
3. **Type Detection**: Transformation checks @type (handling both string and list formats)
4. **Array Handling**: Multiple receiving parties are correctly processed as separate P22/P01/P14 statements
